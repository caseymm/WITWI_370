"No matter what she's wearing, no matter whether she's in a bar, in a dormitory, in the back seat of a car, on a street, drunk or sober, no man has a right to go beyond the word "no". And if she can't consent, it also means no. That too makes it a crime."

This site aims to foster discussion and give perspective to the issue of sexual assault on university campuses across the US. The conversation on sexual assault is a priority topic of the federal government. As a part of a presidential task force report released by the White House> Vice President Joe Biden has begun the first of many "listening sessions" that aim to increase the dialogue about sexual assault on college campuses.

"I'm here to listen. I want you to tell me what you think works on your campuses in your experience, what's not working in your schools, what we should be doing that we aren't doing, what we are doing that is not making much sense."

-Biden

This site is for recent survivors of sexual assault seeking resources and support, past survivors seeking an outlet to tell their story and hear the stories of others, individuals who want to be educated on the issue of sexual assault.

Survivor Stories: This site aims to create a forum for survivors of sexual assault. Those that come forward are courageous for sharing their story, we applaud their frankness and honesty. Individuals input their stories of sexual assault, either identifying themselves or remaining anonymous, and this site moderates these posts.
